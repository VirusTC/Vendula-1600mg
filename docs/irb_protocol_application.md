# Institutional Review Board (IRB) Protocol Application
**Protocol Title:** Evaluation of Oral Systemic Volatile Botanical Monomer Excretion for Spatial Vector Repulsion and Post-Bite Pathological Mitigation  
**Regulatory Status:** IND-Exempt / Exempt Botanical Ingredient Evaluation (45 CFR 46)  
**Lead Investigator:** [Insert Principal Investigator Name]  

## 1. Study Rationale & Objective
Traditional topical repellents fail due to user non-compliance, sweat-induced wash-off, and mechanical abrasion. This protocol evaluates an oral, single-ingredient botanical monomer that undergoes systemic dermal excretion via eccrine/apocrine glands, creating a continuous, zero-washout spatial repulsion zone. Concurrently, the study tracks the ingredient's secondary mechanism: mitigating localized tissue hyperplastic remodeling and inflammatory cascades if a breakthrough vector bite occurs.

## 2. Human Subject Selection & Safety Justification
* **Cohort Size:** $N = 150$ healthy adult volunteers deployed to or residing in high-burden tropical pathogen zones.
* **Inclusion Criteria:** Age 18–60; documented informed consent; clearance of hepatic and renal baselines.
* **Exclusion Criteria:** Pregnancy/lactation; known hypersensitivity to the target botanical family; pre-existing severe dermatological pathologies (e.g., active psoriasis, atopic dermatitis).
* **IND-Exempt Justification:** The active ingredient is a single-ingredient plant derivative historically cataloged as Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) or currently marketed as a food/dietary supplement. It does not introduce novel chemical entities into human subjects, validating an expedited IRB review track.

## 3. Risk/Benefit Analysis
* **Minimal Risks:** Potential transient alteration of personal body odor during peak sweat excretion; mild gastrointestinal discomfort if taken on an empty stomach.
* **Direct Benefits:** Continuous, compliance-free protection against high-burden tropical vectors (e.g., *Anopheles*, *Aedes*) and cellular-level protection against vector-saliva-induced tissue pathologies.
