# Clinical Mechanisms: Botanical Vector-Repulsion & Post-Exposure Treatment
**Entity:** Single-Ingredient Plant-Derived Volatile Monoterpene/Sesquiterpene Extract

## 1. Systemic Dermal Excretion (Pre-Exposure)
The oral botanical compound escapes complete hepatic first-pass metabolism due to its specific lipophilic structures. It is systemically distributed to the eccrine and apocrine glands, where it continuously partitions into human sweat.
* **Zero Wash-Off Profile:** Because the compound is continuously delivered from internal tissue reservoirs out through the pores, it cannot be rubbed off, washed away by water, or degraded by environmental friction.
* **Spatial Olfactory Masking:** Once excreted onto the epidermis, the natural volatile compound evaporates, creating an invisible biochemical shield. It binds to and saturates the mosquito's Odorant-Binding Proteins (OBPs), completely blocking their ability to detect human chemical cues like L-lactic acid and carbon dioxide.

## 2. Onco-Pathological Cascades (Post-Bite Mitigation)
When administered immediately after a vector bite, the botanical ingredient interrupts the local tissue-remodeling cascade initiated by mosquito saliva. Vector saliva contains highly immunogenic proteins that induce severe mast-cell degranulation, micro-vascular remodeling, and hyperplastic tissue changes. If left unmanaged, chronic inflammation at these localized puncture zones can trigger pathological cellular transformation.
