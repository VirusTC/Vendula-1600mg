Complete List of Qualifying Jurisdictions

The following sovereign nations, territories, and regional operational zones qualify under the continuous high-burden vector criteria established by the CDC and the FDA: [[1](https://www.raps.org/resource/regulatory-explainer-everything-you-need-to-know-1.html), [2](https://www.cdc.gov/dengue/areas-with-risk/index.html)]

1\. Sub-Saharan, Eastern, and Central Africa

-   **Frequent/Continuous Risk Zones:** Angola, Benin, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Congo (Brazzaville), Côte d'Ivoire, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Djibouti, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Gabon, Gambia, Ghana, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Kenya, Liberia, Madagascar, Malawi, Mali, Mauritania, Mauritius, Mayotte (France), Mozambique, Namibia, Niger, Nigeria, Réunion (France), Rwanda, Senegal, Seychelles, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Tanzania, Togo, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe. [[1](https://www.cdc.gov/yellow-book/hcp/preparing-international-travelers/yellow-fever-vaccine-and-malaria-prevention-information-by-country.html), [2](https://www.cdc.gov/dengue/areas-with-risk/index.html), [3](https://tripprep.com/library/yellow-fever/events/yellow-fever-risk-list)]

2\. Central America, South America, and the Caribbean

-   **Frequent/Continuous Risk Zones:** Anguilla, Antigua and Barbuda, Argentina (northern endemic zones), Aruba, Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, Bolivia, Brazil, British Virgin Islands, Cayman Islands, Colombia, Costa Rica, Cuba, Dominica, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, El Salvador, French Guiana, Grenada, Guadeloupe, Guatemala, Guyana, Haiti, Honduras, Jamaica, Martinique, Mexico (southern tropical states), Nicaragua, Panama, Paraguay, Peru, Puerto Rico (U.S.), Saint Barthélemy, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Martin, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Sint Maarten, Suriname, Trinidad and Tobago, Turks and Caicos, United States Virgin Islands, Venezuela. [[1](https://www.cdc.gov/yellow-book/hcp/travel-associated-infections-diseases/yellow-fever.html), [2](https://www.cdc.gov/dengue/areas-with-risk/index.html)]

3\. South and Southeast Asia

-   **Frequent/Continuous Risk Zones:** Bangladesh, Bhutan, Cambodia, India, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Maldives, Myanmar (Burma), Nepal, Pakistan, Philippines, Singapore, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Timor-Leste, Vietnam. [[1](https://www.cdc.gov/dengue/areas-with-risk/index.html), [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12925428/)]

4\. Oceania and the Pacific Islands

-   **Frequent/Continuous Risk Zones:** American Samoa (U.S.), Cook Islands, Fiji, French Polynesia, Guam (U.S.), Kiribati, Marshall Islands, Micronesia (Federated States of), Nauru, New Caledonia, Palau, Papua New Guinea, Samoa, Solomon Islands, Tonga, Tuvalu, Vanuatu, Wallis and Futuna. [[1](https://www.cdc.gov/yellow-book/hcp/preparing-international-travelers/yellow-fever-vaccine-and-malaria-prevention-information-by-country.html), [2](https://www.cdc.gov/dengue/areas-with-risk/index.html)]

Domestic / High-Income Exceptions Framework

While developed nations generally do not qualify on a macroeconomic scale, Section 524 of the FD&C Act permits **localized exceptions** if a vector threat expands geographically: [[1](https://www.raps.org/resource/regulatory-explainer-everything-you-need-to-know-1.html)]

-   **The U.S. Gulf Coast Exception:** Endemic pockets within Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, and Florida qualify for localized IRB protocols during seasonal vector surges due to the rapid spread of*Aedes albopictus* carrying Dengue or Zika. [[1](https://www.raps.org/resource/regulatory-explainer-everything-you-need-to-know-1.html), [2](https://www.researchamerica.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/ResearchAmerica-Neglected-Tropical-Diseases-in-the-United-States.pdf)]
