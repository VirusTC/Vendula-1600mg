# Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) Operational Charter
**Protocol Reference:** VVP-PREP-001  
**Framework Jurisdiction:** IND-Exempt / Expanded Access Hybrid Oversight Committee

## 1. Objectives & Autonomy
The DSMB is established as an independent advisory body responsible for assessing the progressive safety, data validity, and clinical endpoints of the oral volatile botanical countermeasure study. The board functions independently of the Sponsor, Principal Investigators, and Institutional Review Boards to eliminate potential conflicts of interest.

## 2. Statistical Review & Stopping Rules
The DSMB will perform scheduled safety data cuts at $N=25$, $N=75$, and $N=150$ enrolled subjects. The trial will face an automatic mandatory operational pause if any of the following stopping boundaries are breached:
* **Safety Threshold:** $\ge 3\%$ of the active cohort experiences a Grade 3 or Grade 4 Adverse Event (via CTCAE v5.0) definitively attributed to the oral botanical compound.
* **Dermal Toxicity Limit:** $\ge 5\%$ of participants exhibit severe exfoliative dermatitis or systemic photo-hypersensitivity at the skin surface where sweat volatilization occurs.
* **Futility Threshold:** Analytical confirmation that sweat excretion flux ($J_{sweat}$) drops below $0.5 \mu\text{g}/\text{cm}^2/\text{hr}$ across $\ge 30\%$ of compliant participants, rendering the spatial olfactory boundary layer functionally ineffective.

## 3. Board Composition Requirements
The board consists of three voting members: one independent biostatistician, one board-certified toxicologist specializing in botanical monographs, and one infectious disease clinician specializing in high-burden tropical medicine.
